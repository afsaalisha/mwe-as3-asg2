HOW TO AVOID IMPULSE BUYING IMPULSE BUYING Tips to Avoid Impulse Buying
  • 1. Make a Shopping List: Before going shopping, make a list of items you need and stick to it to avoid buying unnecessary items on impulse.

  • 2. Set a Budget: Determine a budget for your shopping trip and avoid deviating from it by only purchasing items within your budget.

  • 3. Wait Before Buying: When you feel the urge to buy something on impulse, wait for at least 24 hours before making the purchase. This will give you time to reconsider if it's a necessity or just a whim.

  • 4. Avoid Shopping When Emotional: Avoid shopping when you're feeling stressed, sad, or bored, as emotions can lead to impulsive decisions.

  • 5. Use Cash Instead of Cards: Use cash for your purchases instead of credit or debit cards to make you more aware of your spending and less likely to make impulse purchases.

  • 6. Avoid Sales and Discounts Temptation: Don't fall for sales or discounts unless the item is something you planned to buy beforehand. Sales and discounts often lead to impulse purchases of unnecessary items.
  • 7. Consider the True Cost: Before making a purchase, consider the true cost of the item, including maintenance, storage, and potential resale value.

  • 8. Focus on Needs, Not Wants: Differentiate between needs and wants, and prioritize spending on essential items rather than indulging in impulse purchases of non-essential items.

  • 9. Find Alternatives to Shopping: Instead of shopping for leisure or entertainment, find alternative activities like exercising, reading, or spending time with friends and family.

  • 10. Reflect on Past Impulse Buys: Reflect on past impulse purchases and their impact on your finances to motivate yourself to avoid similar mistakes in the future.
